## Post 1

28 publishers rejected this lawyer's first book. Four years later, his second book sold 7 million copies. And for the next 47 weeks, "The Firm" by John Grisham lived on the NYT's bestsellers list.

Grisham is proof you don't need natural talent to become a wealthy writer. You just have to improve your craft.

Here are 5 of his best writing insights:

1. Grisham sets himself tough creative constraints: Start a novel on Jan 1st, write daily for 3 hours, finish it by July 1st. The key? His tightly controlled writing environment.

2. Everything is secondary to the plot. "I always try to tell a good story... That is my first and primary goal."

3. Ignore the critics. His success meant he would never be critically acclaimed: "Once you sell a lot of books and you are labeled a bestselling author, the serious critics are never gonna say anything nice about you."

4. Write from experience. He writes legal thrillers because he was once a lawyer himself.

5. Even after his wild success, he doesn't see himself as a talented writer: "I cannot write as well as some people; my talent is in coming up with good stories about lawyers."
