## Post 2

It is freaking HARD to hire the right people, train, build a culture, find product market fit, set the right expectations, be able to service customers in the right way, handle feedback, change the menu, raise prices, keep the lights on, organize the books, figure out how to pay and get paid, taxes — IT IS A LOT.

And then you have to find ways to stay relevant with AI, stay up to speed on AI, all the things.

To build a reliable product/service is no small feat. Much respect to the Founders and Business Owners. The past 4 years running Exit Five has shed light on so many things I did not see before.

## Post 4

Spoiler alert: Everything in marketing works.

I've done over 300 hours of interviews with CMOs and VPs in B2B marketing. I spent a decade as a marketing leader myself and I can tell you this one thing: Everything in marketing works.

YouTube works. LinkedIn works. Direct mail works. Webinars work. TikTok works. Email works. Events work. PR works. Partnerships work.

But the challenge is figuring out which channels can work for you with the ingredients you have. You don't always have the product, the features, the timing, the wind at your back, the talent on the team, the budget.

Too often we try to do everything and the result is another B2B YouTube channel with 30 videos that all have between 17 and 62 views. You'd be better off picking one channel and doing DEEP there and then finding ways to stack those wins into other channels.

And last thing - this is the hardest one. Often times GOOD MARKETING is not good for the company. It's harder to measure, takes time, not obvious, doesn't have UTM parameters. And that's what makes it good and makes it work, but you need to have the patience to trust it.

Treat your job like a craft. Have conviction. Don't follow best practices. Use your brain. Be creative. More important now than ever when everyone has access to the Slop Machine.
